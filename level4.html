Level 4

Map a Workflow AI Can Help With

AI works best inside a clear process. This level helps you map the steps of a real task so AI fits into your actual work.
What you will have by the end:One mapped workflow showing where AI fits into a real task
The lesson

Why workflows matter

Using AI for a one-off task is useful. Using AI inside a repeatable workflow is where the real time savings come from.

A workflow is just the sequence of steps you take to complete a task. Most work tasks already have a workflow — you just do it without thinking about it. This level makes that workflow visible so you can see exactly where AI fits.

The goal is not to hand the whole task to AI. The goal is to identify the one or two steps inside the task where AI genuinely helps — and build a consistent habit around those steps.

How to map a workflow

Breaking a task into steps

Take the task you identified in Level 1. Think about everything you do from start to finish to complete that task. Write each step down.

Most tasks have between three and eight steps when you break them down. Here is an example:

Start
Client meeting happens
Step 1
Review notes from the meeting
Step 2 ★ AI fits here
Draft follow-up email
Step 3
Review and edit the draft
Step 4
Send the email
Done
File notes for next time

★ The starred step is where the GOAL message from Level 2 gets used.

Finding the AI fit

Where does AI actually help?

Once you have your steps mapped, look for steps that match these patterns:

Writing and drafting
Any step where you are producing words — emails, summaries, reports, responses
Organising information
Any step where you are sorting, categorising, or structuring content
Generating options
Any step where you are brainstorming or need a starting point
Explaining or simplifying
Any step where you need to understand something complex before moving forward
What to keep in mind

A few workflow principles

Start with one step. You do not need to use AI for every step. Pick the one step where it helps most and build the habit there first.

You stay in control. AI handles a step — you review the output, make changes, and move to the next step. The workflow is still yours.

The workflow can evolve. Once the first AI step feels natural, you can look at adding a second. Gradual is fine.

Your Level 4 activity

Map your workflow

Take your Level 1 task and map it out step by step. Then identify where AI fits. Submit your workflow map using the activity form below.

  1. 1
    Write out the steps
    Take your Level 1 task and write every step you take to complete it from start to finish. Aim for three to eight steps.
  2. 2
    Mark where AI fits
    Look at each step and mark it: AI helps here / AI does not help here / Not sure. Use the patterns from the lesson above as a guide.
  3. 3
    Write one AI step in detail
    Pick the step where AI helps most. Write what you would ask AI to do at that step — this can be your GOAL message from Level 2.
  4. 4
    Submit your workflow map
    Paste your step list and your marked AI step into the activity form below.
