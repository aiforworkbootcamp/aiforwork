What a personal AI workflow looks like

Your workflow has four parts — one from each level you completed. Here is what each part contains:

Part 1
Your use case
One sentence describing the task and why AI helps with it
Part 2
Your GOAL message
The structured message you wrote in Level 2, refined if needed
Part 3
Your checking checklist
The three to five questions you wrote in Level 3
Part 4
Your workflow map
The step-by-step process from Level 4 with AI's role marked
After this course

What to do with your workflow

Use it. The workflow only has value if you actually apply it to your real work. Try it this week.

Adjust it. After a few uses, you will know what to change. Update your GOAL message, refine your checklist, add a step to the workflow.

Build from it. Once this workflow feels natural, apply the same process to a second task. The four-step method works for any word-based task.

You started this course as a beginner. You are finishing it with a real, working AI system for your own work. That is not a small thing.

Your Level 5 activity

Build your final workflow

Complete the steps below to assemble your personal AI workflow. Then submit it using the activity form — this is your Level 5 artifact and the first step toward completing the beta.

  1. 1
    Gather your four pieces
    Pull together: your Level 1 use case, your Level 2 GOAL message, your Level 3 checking checklist, and your Level 4 workflow map.
  2. 2
    Write your complete workflow
    Combine the four pieces into one clear document. Give it a title. Format it however makes sense to you — a list, a short document, a table.
  3. 3
    Read it through once
    Does it describe something you would actually do? If anything feels off, adjust it now. This is your workflow — it should fit your real work.
  4. 4
    Submit it
    Paste your complete workflow into the activity form below. This is your final course artifact.
