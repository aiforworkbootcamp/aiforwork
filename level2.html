Build a Better Work Prompt

Most people type vague questions into AI and get vague answers back. This level fixes that.

The lesson

Why your message to AI matters

AI tools do not read your mind. They respond to exactly what you give them. A vague message gets a vague response. A clear, structured message gets something you can actually use.

Most beginners type something like: "Help me with my emails." That gives AI almost nothing to work with. Compare that to: "I need to write a follow-up email to a client after our meeting today. The tone should be professional but warm. Keep it under 150 words."

The second message gives AI a task, a context, a tone, and a constraint. That is the difference between a useful response and a generic one.

The framework

The GOAL message framework

GOAL is a simple four-part structure for writing clear AI messages. You do not need to use every part every time — but knowing all four helps you build better messages faster.

G — Goal
What do you want AI to do?
e.g. Draft, summarise, explain, create, organise, rewrite...
O — Output
What should the result look like?
e.g. An email, a bullet list, a short paragraph, a table...
A — Audience / Context
Who is this for or what is the situation?
e.g. A client, a colleague, a job application, a weekly report...
L — Limits
Any constraints on the response?
e.g. Under 150 words, professional tone, no jargon, use UK spelling...
Putting it together

What a GOAL message looks like

Here is the same task written without and with the GOAL framework:

Without GOAL
"Help me with my emails."
With GOAL
"Draft a follow-up email to a client after our project kick-off meeting today. It should be professional but warm in tone. Keep it under 150 words and end with a clear next step."
What to know

A few things to keep in mind

AI is not a search engine. You are not looking up a fact — you are giving it a task. The more specific your task, the better the result.

You can always adjust. If the first response is not quite right, add more detail or change one part of your message. AI works through conversation, not single questions.

Check what it gives you. Even a well-written message can produce an imperfect response. You will learn how to check responses properly in Level 3.

Your Level 2 activity

Write your first GOAL message

Use the task you identified in Level 1 to write your first structured AI message. Follow the steps below, then submit it using the activity form.

  1. 1
    Take out your Level 1 task
    The task you identified in Level 1 — the repetitive, word-based work task. That is what you are building a message for.
  2. 2
    Write your GOAL message
    Use the four parts: what is your Goal, what is the Output you want, what is the Audience or context, and what Limits apply (length, tone, format)?
  3. 3
    Test it
    Paste your message into a free AI tool — ChatGPT (chat.openai.com) or Claude (claude.ai). See what comes back.
  4. 4
    Submit your message
    Paste your GOAL message into the activity form below. You do not need to submit the AI response — just the message you wrote.
