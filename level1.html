The lesson

What this level is about

A lot of people try AI by asking it random questions and then decide it is not useful. That is not a fair test. AI works best when you give it a specific, real task — not a vague experiment.

This level helps you identify one real task from your own work or life where AI could genuinely help. Not a made-up example. Your actual work.

That task becomes the foundation for everything you do in the rest of the course.

What to know first

What AI is actually good at

AI tools like ChatGPT, Claude, and others are not search engines and they are not magic. They are text tools. They are good at specific things and not good at others.

AI tends to work well for tasks that involve words, structure, or thinking through options.

Here are examples of the kinds of tasks AI handles well:

Writing
Drafting emails, summarising documents, rewriting something in a clearer way
Planning
Breaking a project into steps, creating an agenda, thinking through options
Research support
Getting a plain-English explanation of something, generating a list of questions to explore
Templates
Creating a first draft of a recurring document, report, or message you write often
Important: AI can be wrong. It can sound confident and still give you incorrect information. You should always check AI responses before acting on them — especially for facts, numbers, or anything that matters. You will learn how to do this in Level 3.
Finding your task

How to find your AI starting point

Think about your typical work week. You are looking for a task that meets these three conditions:

  • It involves words — writing, reading, summarising, explaining, or organising information
  • You do it more than once — it is something that comes up regularly, not a one-off
  • It takes more time or energy than it should — it feels repetitive, slow, or draining

Here are some real examples from different types of work:

Example
Writing follow-up emails after client meetings
Example
Summarising long reports or documents before a meeting
Example
Creating a weekly update or status report
Example
Drafting responses to common customer or colleague questions
Example
Researching a topic and pulling out the key points
Example
Turning rough notes into a clear document or agenda
Tip: Do not try to pick the perfect task. Pick a real one. You can always change it later. The goal right now is to have something specific and personal to work with.
Your Level 1 activity

Find your starting point

Use the steps below to identify your one AI starting task. Then fill in the activity form to capture it. This becomes your first course artifact — you will use it in every level that follows.

  1. 1
    Think of 2–3 tasks from your real work week that involve words and happen regularly.
  2. 2
    Pick the one that feels most repetitive or time-consuming.
  3. 3
    Write it down in one clear sentence. Example: "I write follow-up emails after every client call and it takes me 20 minutes each time."

    Submit it using the form below. Keep your answer — you will use it in Level 2.
